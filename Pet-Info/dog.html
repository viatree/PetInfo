  • Characteristics of dog


    Dogs are predators and meat-eating animals, have sharp teeth and strong jaws for attacking, biting, and tearing food. Dogs vary greatly in size, appearance and behavior compared to other pets. Dogs have strong muscles, united ankle bones, cardiovascular systems that support physical endurance and running speed, and teeth to catch and tear prey.

    The sense of sight, For dogs, color is a subliminal signal that is captured to distinguish shapes from overlapping objects, and not color to objects that can be distinguished directly by dogs. According to research, dogs can see various shades of yellow, purple or violet, ultra violet. A dog's eyepiece is flatter than a human eye lens, so dogs are less able to see in detail than humans. Conversely, the dog's eyes are more sensitive to light and movement than the human eye.

    The sense of hearing. Dogs can hear low frequency sounds of 16 Hz to 70 KHz. The number of widths of this frequency is quite good, but still less than hearing cats. The dog is able to determine the source of sound faster than humans, while at the same time be able to hear sounds whose sources are four times more distant that humans can hear.

    The sense of smell, Dogs have almost 220 million olfactory cells that are sensitive to odors. According to the research results, dogs can distinguish two types of odors: odor particles in the air that spread from people or objects, and odor particles in the ground that can still be detected after a while. The characteristics of the two types of odor particles seem to be quite different. Odor particles in the air easily disappear, but may be so clear and not mixed with other odors, whereas odor particles in the soil are relatively more permanent.

  • Dog food


    Some veterinarians are currently debating pet dogs classified as omnivorous or carnivorous based on the food eaten. Classification into a carnivorous order does not mean that dogs should only eat meat. Dogs can digest well a variety of foods, including vegetables and cereals that can be consumed by dogs in large portions. The plants are eaten by wild dogs to meet the needs of amino acids. Pet dogs can survive healthy only with vegetarian food mixed well, especially those containing milk and eggs. But some sources even doubt this, vegetarian dogs are feared to experience enlarged cardiac cardiomyopathy heart muscle due to a lack of amino acid L-carnitine. Even so, dogs can stay healthy if given balanced nutrition.


    Some foods that humans normally enjoy can be fatal to dogs, including chocolate (theobromine poisoning), onions (onions), grapes, raisins, certain types of gum, certain artificial sweeteners, and macadamia nuts. It is now well known that cocoa is a dangerous substance for dogs, while white chocolate may not be harmful.
