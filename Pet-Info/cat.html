  • Characteristics Of Cats

    Cats are one of the greatest predators in the world. But because of its small size, cats are not so dangerous for humans. The only danger that can arise is the possibility of rabies infection due to cat bites and scratches from the cat's nails are very painful and painful.

    Cats are considered "perfect carnivores" with special teeth and digestive tracts. Unlike other carnivores, cats eat almost nothing that contains plants.

    Pet cats that live indoors should be given a litter box filled with sand or special material sold at pet stores. There should also be a special place for cats to scratch. This is important because cats need this clawing activity to remove the old layer on their nails so that the nails can stay sharp and maintain their health. The absence of this special place will cause many cats to damage the furniture.

    Cat is included to the Clean animals. They often take care of themselves by licking their hair. Their saliva or saliva is a powerful cleaning agent, but can trigger allergies in humans. Sometimes cats spit hairball or coils of hair that collect in their stomachs. Cats can see in very bright light. When there is too little light to see, the cat will use its "whiskers" or vibration (vibrissae) to help it determine direction and become an additional sense organ.

    Cats store energy by sleeping more often than other animals. Cat sleep duration varies between 12-16 hours per day, with an average number of 13-14 hours. But it is not uncommon to find cats who sleep for 20 hours in one day.

  • Cat food

    Cats often show food-picking behavior. This is because they have a special olfactory organ on the roof of their mouth called the vomeronasal organ or Jacobson's organ. When this organ is stimulated by a certain type of food, the cat will reject food other than that food.


    Dried food

    Dried food (containing 8-10% water) is usually made by extruding meat with high pressure and temperature. Dried grains have a much lower price compared to other types of cat food. Another advantage of this dry food is, this food is good for dental health and this food is also not easily damaged and the taste can last for several days, even if left in a cat food bowl.


    Wet food

    Wet or canned food (containing 75-78% water) generally comes in various sizes of cans, which are 3 ounces (85 grams), 5.5 ounces (156 grams), and 13 ounces (369 grams). These foods are made from meat high quality. Wet food is made by grinding and mixed and then ground to a pulp. Wet food has been systematically sterilized and pasteurized. High water content from these foods contains many health benefits, but these foods are not good for dental health. Compared with dry food, wet food can reduce the likelihood of diabetes urination problems, kidney failure, constipation and obesity in cats.


    Vegetarian food

    Vegetarian food or vegan food is available for vegetarian cats. Vegetarian food is enriched with a variety of nutrients, such as taurine and arachidonic acid, which shows that cats are obligate carnivorous animals, which cannot synthesize food from plants. Some vegetarian cat food brands have been labeled by AAFOO (Association of American Feed Control Officials) by their manufacturers, which means that these foods meet the nutritional profile of cat food.


    Homemade food

    Many pet owners provide homemade food for their pets. These foods generally consist of several forms, such as raw or cooked meat, bones, vegetables and supplements, such as taurine and multivitamins.
